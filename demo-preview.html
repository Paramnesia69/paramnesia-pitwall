Ambient & Page Feel

01
AmbientBackground — series-reactive orbs
The primary orb is hardcoded #E10600, the secondary #0090D4. Switching to MotoGP, DTM, or WRC changes nothing about the room. Setting --pw-series-accent on <html> when activeFilter changes, and reading it in AmbientBackground for the orb color and the top-edge gradient (also hardcoded F1 red), makes every series feel like its own space. The CSS var hook is already defined in globals.css — it is just not consumed anywhere.

Hero Card

02
Circuit map as HeroCard background
getCircuitImage(name) in src/lib/images.ts is only used inside CircuitStatsPanel, deep in the detail overlay's scroll. The HeroCard is the highest-visibility surface — a 320px+ glass panel — and its background is purely a gradient + corner glow. Placing the circuit map at opacity: 0.07, object-fit: contain, mix-blend-mode: screen on the right half would be the most cinematic single change available. Degrades gracefully for non-F1 circuits where getCircuitImage returns undefined.

Series Navigation

04
Sticky series nav with glass blur
The series filter bar in Dashboard.tsx:148 is inside normal document flow and scrolls away permanently. For a command center, losing the series context while browsing results and standings is a UX hole. position: sticky; top: 0; z-index: var(--pw-z-nav) with backdrop-filter: blur(20px) and a matching background: rgba(6,6,11,0.85) makes it float as a persistent glass tab bar. --pw-z-nav: 100 is already defined and reserved for exactly this.

A mask-image: linear-gradient(to right, black 88%, transparent 100%) on the nav wrapper is a single CSS line — signals overflow without a scrollbar and stays true to the glassmorphism aesthetic.

Sparklines for non-F1 series
